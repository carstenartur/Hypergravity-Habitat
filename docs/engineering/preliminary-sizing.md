Preliminary Sizing Study

Project: Hypergravity Habitat
Document: Preliminary Engineering Sizing
Version: 0.1 (Draft)

⸻

Purpose

This document provides first-order engineering estimates for potential Hypergravity Habitat configurations.

The objective is not to optimise a final design, but to compare different infrastructure concepts regarding

* achievable gravity,
* operating speed,
* required infrastructure,
* estimated construction costs,
* expected operating costs,
* maintainability,
* scalability.

Unless otherwise stated, all calculations assume standard Earth gravity.

⸻

Assumptions

Target Hypergravity

Two target environments are considered.

Condition	Effective gravity
Moderate	1.10 g
Enhanced	1.25 g

These values were selected because they are expected to be suitable for long-duration physiological research while remaining well below the acceleration levels encountered in aerospace medicine.

Concept C – Continuous Railway Ring

Entire ring occupied by coupled railway vehicles.

Advantages

* minimal aerodynamic drag
* large usable volume
* scalable
* multiple laboratories
* private accommodation

Disadvantages

* many vehicles
* wheel maintenance
* rail maintenance

Estimated investment

€180–350 million

Scalability

The proposed infrastructure can evolve in several stages.

Stage	Description
1	Three-coach demonstrator
2	Research train
3	Continuous railway habitat
4	Dual-track research facility
5	Maglev-based international infrastructure

This staged approach allows early scientific studies using largely existing railway technology while preserving the possibility of evolving toward a larger international research infrastructure if scientific demand justifies the investment.

⸻

Remarks

The values presented in this document are preliminary engineering estimates intended to support feasibility discussions. They are not suitable for procurement or construction planning and should be refined through detailed engineering studies, consultation with railway and maglev experts, and validation against operational experience.
