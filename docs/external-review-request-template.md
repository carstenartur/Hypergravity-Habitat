## Short Message

Dear [Name],

I am preparing an open research documentation project called **Hypergravity Habitat**. It asks whether sustained moderate hypergravity above Earth-normal gravity could become a useful terrestrial research environment for biological, human-centred, and technical systems.

The project is explicitly **not** a construction proposal and does not claim that human exposure, clinical use, athlete training, or a full facility is ready or justified. The current goal is to test the research gap, assumptions, safety logic, and smallest useful demonstrator path.

I would value critical feedback on one or more of the following questions:

1. Is the proposed research gap real, overstated, or already covered by existing facilities?
2. Which literature or facilities are missing from the review?
3. Which assumptions are weak or wrong?
4. Is a payload-first demonstrator the right first step?
5. Which safety, ethics, data, or engineering issue is underestimated?
6. What evidence would justify stopping or narrowing the project?
