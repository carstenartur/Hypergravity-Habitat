# Cost Model

**Project:** Hypergravity Habitat  
**Document:** Cost Model  
**Version:** 0.1 (Draft)

---

# Purpose

This document defines the preliminary cost model used throughout the Hypergravity Habitat project.

Rather than presenting a single construction cost, the objective is to identify the principal cost drivers, compare different infrastructure concepts and provide a transparent basis for future refinement.

All values presented here are preliminary engineering estimates.

---

# Cost Categories

Project costs are divided into the following categories.

## Capital Expenditure (CAPEX)

One-time investment costs.

Examples:

- land acquisition
- earthworks
- railway or guideway construction
- electrical infrastructure
- buildings
- rolling stock
- laboratories
- medical equipment
- engineering
- commissioning

---

## Operating Expenditure (OPEX)

Recurring annual costs.

Examples:

- electricity
- personnel
- maintenance
- inspections
- insurance
- administration
- scientific operation

---

## Renewal Costs

Infrastructure replacement during project lifetime.

Examples:

- rail replacement
- wheel replacement
- bearings
- traction equipment
- transformers
- signalling equipment
- building refurbishment

---

# Railway Cost Drivers

## Infrastructure

Primary cost drivers include

- track length
- earthworks
- bridges (if required)
- drainage
- fencing
- workshop
- depot
- electrical systems

Track construction cost scales approximately linearly with total track length.

---

## Rolling Stock

The demonstrator concept assumes maximum reuse of existing railway technology.

Potential procurement options include

- surplus passenger coaches
- refurbished regional trains
- refurbished locomotives

Vehicle refurbishment may represent a larger cost than procurement itself.

---

## Continuous Operation

Unlike conventional passenger railways, Hypergravity Habitat is expected to operate continuously.

Consequences include

- increased wheel wear
- increased rail wear
- increased bearing wear
- higher electricity consumption
- increased maintenance frequency

---

# Maglev Cost Drivers

Potential advantages

- no wheel wear
- no rail wear
- reduced vibration
- lower mechanical maintenance

Potential disadvantages

- expensive guideway
- expensive power electronics
- proprietary technology
- limited supplier base

---

# Personnel

Typical personnel categories may include

- train operators
- maintenance technicians
- electricians
- medical staff
- researchers
- laboratory technicians
- administration
- safety personnel

Personnel cost is expected to become one of the dominant operating expenses.

---

# Electricity

Continuous operation requires permanent electrical power.

Consumers include

- traction
- HVAC
- lighting
- laboratories
- computing
- communications
- medical equipment

Future revisions should derive electricity demand from

- vehicle mass
- operating speed
- aerodynamic drag
- rolling resistance
- HVAC load
- laboratory equipment

---

# Maintenance Model

Maintenance should distinguish between

## Preventive maintenance

Scheduled inspections and component replacement.

---

## Corrective maintenance

Unexpected failures.

---

## Predictive maintenance

Condition-based maintenance using continuous monitoring.

---

# Cost Scaling

Construction cost is expected to increase primarily with

- ring radius
- total track length
- building size
- scientific equipment

Operating cost is expected to increase primarily with

- operating hours
- staffing
- maintenance
- electricity
- number of participants

# Life-Cycle Cost

The project should be evaluated using total life-cycle cost rather than construction cost alone.

Life-cycle cost includes

- construction
- operation
- maintenance
- renewal
- decommissioning

This approach allows comparison between concepts with different investment and maintenance profiles.

# Summary

The purpose of this document is not to predict the final cost of a Hypergravity Habitat.

Instead, it establishes a transparent framework that allows future engineering studies to refine assumptions while maintaining traceability of all cost estimates.
